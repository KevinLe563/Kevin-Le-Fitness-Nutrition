PROBLEMS WITH 2-DAY SPLIT AND FULL BODY WORKOUT

As a beginner, I enjoyed doing a full body workout or 2-day split. However, as I progressed, I began to train harder and harder (progressive overload). The 2-day splits and full body workouts became more and more difficult as the workouts were extremely long. Additionally, due to my growing focus on the compound lifts, it became difficult to improve doing multiple compounds in 1 day. It was difficult to focus on both volume and strength (full body workout lacked volume while it was difficult to focus on strength for the 2-day workout). In order to focus on increasing my lifts on compounds while maintening higher volume, I decided to switch to a PPL split (push pull legs).

WHY 3 DAY SPLIT? (PPL)

A 3 day split allows me to maintain high training volume while shortening the training time. This ensures that I can train harder mentally and physically to progressively overload from workout to workout. Additionally, I'm able to split up my compound lifts (bench, deadlift, and squat) on separate day, which makes sure I am fresh for every compound lift. I am able to focus on both volume/intensity and strength every workout.
